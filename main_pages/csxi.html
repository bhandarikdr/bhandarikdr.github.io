👋 Welcome Message

Dear students, Welcome to Grade 11 Computer Science! As your educator, I am thrilled to guide you through this fascinating journey into the world of computing. Information and Communication Technology (ICT) is rapidly reshaping our world, and this course is specifically designed to provide you with a robust foundation for future studies and entry into the tech job market.

Computer Science is inherently hands-on. To succeed this year, you must understand both the theoretical principles of technology and how to apply them practically. Let’s look at how our course is structured and what is required of you to successfully complete it.

📝 Critical Course Requirement: The Lab Report

To successfully complete this course and be eligible for your practical marks, you are strictly required to submit a comprehensive lab report at the end of the academic year.

This report is a compiled, systematic documentation of all the laboratory experiments, coding challenges, website mockups, and software exercises you perform throughout the term.

What your Lab Report must include:

  • The Writing Process: The step-by-step methodology, algorithms, or setup procedures used for each practical task.
  • The Deliverables: Clean source code (for C programming and HTML/CSS), data sheets, or edited media logs.
  • The Outcome: Proof of working execution (such as program outputs or screenshots of built layouts).

Please do not leave this until the last minute. Treat every lab session as a step toward building your final report.
