👋 Welcome Message

Dear students, Welcome to Grade 12 Computer Science! As your educator, I am thrilled to guide you through this advanced stage of your studies. Building on what you learned last year, this year's curriculum moves into systems architecture, stronger programming logic, relational databases, and computer networking. It's designed to give you the depth you'll need for higher studies and a strong start in the tech industry.

Computer Science is still very much hands-on this year. Doing well depends on pairing solid theory with careful, logical practice in the lab. Let's look at how the course is structured and what's expected of you to complete it successfully.

📊 Scheme of Evaluation

  • Theory Examination (External Board) 50 Marks
  • Practical Assessments (Internal + External) 50 Marks
  • Total Weightage 100 Marks

⚠️ Practical Marks Breakdown

  • Internal Portfolio (Attendance, Labs, Projects) 25 Marks

📝 Critical Course Requirement: The Lab Report

To successfully complete this course and be eligible for your practical marks, you are strictly required to submit a comprehensive lab report at the end of the academic year.

This report is a compiled, systematic documentation of the SQL exercises, C programming solutions, and any other laboratory tasks you complete throughout the term.

What your Lab Report must include:

  • The Design Process: Your query logic, ER diagrams, algorithms, or program design steps used for each practical task.
  • The Deliverables: Clean SQL scripts, C source code, and any supporting data files.
  • The Outcome: Proof of successful execution (query results, program outputs, or screenshots).

Please do not leave this until the last minute. Treat every lab session as a step toward building your final report.
